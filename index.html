SELF-SUPERVISED SPEECH ENHANCEMENT BASED ON SPEAKER VERIFICATION

  Luyao Zhang, Jingjing Yu*, Chi Yang, Lirui Wu

Electronic and Information Engineering, Beijing Jiaotong University

Submitted to 2024 IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing

Fig. 1 Framework of proposed STGCN-SV for self-supervised speech enhancement.

The detials of dynamic multi-dimensional spatial-temporal GCN (STGCN) have been published in reference[1], including the principle of STGCN, training details, and experimental validations.

This paper proposes a self-supervised speech enhancement algorithm based on speaker verification which transforms the supervised STGCN to the unsupervised learnng framework, aiming to achieve robust speech enhancement of target speaker in the absence of ground-truth data and unseen scene conditions. In addition, feature-aware modules are introduced to achieve dynamic environmental adaptation, which is applicable in the unknown multi-source realistic scenes for smart interactive devices. Therefore, our self-supervised framework can denoise, suppress interfering persons and enhance the unfamiliar target by the few-shot speaker verification for unknown dynamic scenes. The framework is shown in Fig. 1.

Conclusions:

Table 1 shows the experimental results in four different scenes, where the upper part is the results of supervised models on a general dataset, and the lower part is the result of self-supervised models on a specific speaker dataset.

According to the upper part of Table1:

1) Compared with prior state-of-the art methods, our STGCN_NTA show 25.51%, 18.80% and 28.66% improvements over all scenes.

2) Combining STGCN and noise-aware with contrastive loss, PESQ improves 2.43% and 2.78% compared to the original STGCN in CAF and PED scenes.

3) STGCN with noise-aware and target-aware further improve speech enhancement performance in all noisy scenes, especially in CAF and PED scenes.

Therefore, the experimental results prove that the noise-aware module brings better scene-adaptability for suppressing unseen noise sources, and the target-aware module brings better sensitivity about human speech.

According to the upper part of Table1:

1) Compared with unsupervised baseline method, our STGCN_NTA show 62% improvements over all scenes.

2) Compared with the PESQ of MetricGAN-U-SV is improved by 25% compared with MetricGAN-U, and the STGCN-SV is improved by 2% compared with STGCN.

Therefore, the proposed STGCN-SV can further improve the speech enhancement performance of the network by introducing a few-shot speaker verification module for self-supervised learning.

Conclusions:

1)In the rectangular array of CHiME3, STGCN improves 52% compared with noisy speech, 11% compared with optimal baseline (CNN), and 1 9% compared with UNet-GCN.

2)In the circle array of cocktail party, STGCN improves 28% compared with noisy speech, 13% compared with optimal baseline (CNN), and 15% compared with UNet-GCN.

3)From the experimental results, it can be demonstrated that STGCN improves the quality of target signal on both rectangular and circular arrays, proving the effectiveness and robustness of our multidimensional Spatial-temporal GCN.
